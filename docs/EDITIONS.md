# Special Editions

> **Status**: Planning phase – editions are not yet available

## What Are Special Editions?

Archilles is being developed as a **modular platform**. The core functionality (what exists now) will always be **free and open source** under the MIT License.

**Special Editions** are discipline-specific commercial extensions that add advanced features for researchers with specialized needs.

## Why Editions?

Different research disciplines have different requirements:
- Historians need timeline views and prosopography
- Literary scholars need motif tracking and intertextual analysis
- Legal researchers need citation networks and precedent tracking
- Musicologists need score integration and theoretical terminology

Rather than bloating the core with niche features, Editions keep the base system lean while offering powerful tools for those who need them.

## Planned Editions

### 📜 Historical Edition

**Target users**: Historians, archivists, philologists

**Features**:
- **Timeline visualization**: Plot events and sources chronologically
- **Prosopography**: Person networks, family trees, social connections
- **Chronology-aware search**: Understand historical periods and dating systems
- **Primary source handling**: Special treatment for charters, letters, chronicles
- **Medieval dating**: Convert between calendar systems
- **Paleography tools**: Integration with manuscript databases
- **Entity disambiguation**: "Constantine" → Constantine I, Constantine VII, etc.

**Pricing**: TBD (target: ~99€ one-time)

### 📖 Literary Edition

**Target users**: Literary scholars, comparative literature researchers

**Features**:
- **Motif tracking**: Identify and trace recurring themes across texts
- **Intertextual connections**: Discover references, allusions, influences
- **Narrative structure analysis**: Plot arcs, character development
- **Character networks**: Who interacts with whom, when, how
- **Stylometric tools**: Authorship attribution, style comparison
- **Genre-aware search**: Understand literary conventions
- **Translation comparison**: Link original texts with translations

**Pricing**: TBD (target: ~99€ one-time)

### ⚖️ Legal Edition

**Target users**: Legal historians, legal researchers

**Features**:
- **Citation networks**: Map case law references
- **Precedent tracking**: Follow legal reasoning through cases
- **Jurisdiction-aware search**: Filter by court, region, legal system
- **Case law handling**: Special parsing for legal citations
- **Legal terminology**: Domain-specific embeddings
- **Statute linking**: Connect cases to statutes and codes
- **Timeline views**: Track legal developments over time

**Pricing**: TBD (target: ~149€ one-time, higher for commercial use)

### 🎵 Musical Edition

**Target users**: Musicologists, music theorists, composers

**Features**:
- **Score analysis integration**: Connect text discussions with musical scores
- **Theoretical terminology**: Domain-specific vocabulary
- **Composer networks**: Influence, teaching lineages, collaborations
- **Period-aware search**: Renaissance, Baroque, Classical, etc.
- **Manuscript handling**: Integration with musical manuscript databases
- **Harmonic analysis tools**: Link theoretical discussions to examples
- **Performance practice**: Treatise mining for historical performance

**Pricing**: TBD (target: ~99€ one-time)

## Business Model

### Core (Free Forever)
- Full-text search (semantic + keyword + hybrid)
- Calibre integration
- All current features
- Regular updates
- MIT License

### Editions (One-Time Purchase)
- Specialized features for your discipline
- Lifetime updates for major version (e.g., v1.x)
- Priority support
- Early access to new edition features

### Why One-Time?
- No subscriptions (you own it)
- No vendor lock-in
- Works offline forever
- Privacy-respecting business model

## Will Core Remain Free?

**Yes, absolutely.** Core functionality will never become paid.

Special Editions extend the platform—they don't replace the core. You can use Archilles forever without buying any edition.

## Why Not Just Make Everything Free?

Building and maintaining sophisticated research tools takes time and expertise. Edition sales:
- Fund ongoing core development
- Support better documentation
- Enable faster feature development
- Keep the project sustainable long-term

We believe in **sustainable open source**—a healthy core with optional commercial extensions.
